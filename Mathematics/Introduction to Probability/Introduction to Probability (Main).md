# Introduction to Probability

*Joseph K. Blitzstein & Jessica Hwang*

_Notes compiled 2026-04-17_

---

## About This Book

A rigorous yet accessible introduction to probability theory, used in Harvard's Statistics 110 course. The book builds from counting and sample spaces up through Markov chains and Markov chain Monte Carlo, with a strong emphasis on intuition and real-world examples.

---

## Chapters

- **[Chapter 1 — Probability and Counting](<Chapter 1 Probability and Counting/Chapter 1 (Main).md>)** — Sample spaces, naive probability, counting techniques, classic problems (birthday problem, de Montmort, Bose-Einstein)

---

## Key Takeaways

- Probability is the mathematics of uncertainty — built on set theory and axioms
- Counting is fundamental: most probability problems reduce to counting carefully
- Intuition is often wrong — the birthday problem and matching problems show this clearly
