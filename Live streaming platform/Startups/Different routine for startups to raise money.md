### Tier 1: The "Normal Routine" (Regulation CF)

_The bread-and-butter of your platform. Capped at $5M. Open to the general public._

**The Vibe:** High energy, community-driven, fast-paced. **The Psychology:** FOMO (Fear Of Missing Out) and social proof. Seeing everyday people invest small amounts creates a snowball effect.

- **Pre-Stream (Stacking the Deck):** The startup files their Form C with the SEC via your platform's backend. They quietly secure 20% to 30% of their funding goal from friends, family, and early believers off-camera. You _never_ let a founder go live with a $0 progress bar; people want to join a winning hand, not be the first to risk their chips.
    
- **The Live Stream (The Main Event):**
    
    - **The Hook (First 10 mins):** The founder pitches the vision and demonstrates the product live.
        
    - **The AMA (30 mins):** This is where the money is made. The founder answers questions live from the chat. The audience is actively reading the founder's body language—looking for hesitation, confidence, or defensive cues when asked tough questions about cash flow or competition.
        
    - **The Call to Action:** As the progress bar ticks up, the founder calls out milestones ("We just hit $100k!"). Your platform enforces the individual 5% or 10% investment limits dynamically in the background so no one over-commits legally.
        
- **Post-Stream:** The SEC's 48-hour cooling-off period begins. The founder must keep the community engaged via updates so hyped-up investors don't second-guess their decision and pull their money off the table.

### Tier 2: The "Whale Stream" (Regulation D, Rule 506c)

_Unlimited capital. Only verified wealthy individuals (Accredited Investors)._

**The Vibe:** Exclusive, analytical, high-stakes. **The Psychology:** Authority, scarcity, and calculated risk. These investors aren't swayed by a flashy video; they are looking at the math and the founder's grit.

- **Pre-Stream (The Velvet Rope):** Your platform heavily markets the upcoming pitch, but viewers must pass a strict financial verification gate (proving $1M+ net worth or $200k+ income) just to enter the live room.
    
- **The Live Stream (The Deep Dive):**
    
    - **Data Overlays:** While the founder talks, the stream displays live dashboards of their Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Lifetime Value (LTV), and burn rate.
        
    - **The "Shark" Panel:** You invite 2 or 3 well-known angel investors to join the stream on camera. They grill the founder live. The audience of accredited investors watches this interaction closely. They are reading the dynamic—does the founder fold under pressure, or do they hold their ground?
        
    - **The Close:** Minimum investment limits are high (e.g., $10,000 minimum). The urgency comes from the round filling up quickly, shutting out slow movers.
        
- **Post-Stream:** Immediate follow-up with legal paperwork (Subscription Agreements) to lock in the verbal commitments made during the stream.

### Tier 3: The "Blockbuster" (Regulation A+ Tier 2)

_Raising up to $75M. Open to the public, but requires a massive SEC audit (a "Mini-IPO")._

**The Vibe:** Highly polished, corporate, theatrical. **The Psychology:** Momentum and inevitable scale.

- **Pre-Stream (Months of Prep):** The startup spends $100k+ on SEC audits and legal fees. Your platform runs a "Testing the Waters" campaign—essentially a massive marketing push where people can "reserve" shares without putting money down, allowing the founder to gauge the odds of a successful raise before committing.
    
- **The Live Stream (The Telethon):**
    
    - **High Production:** This isn't a webcam from a bedroom. This is a multi-camera studio setup.
        
    - **Guest Appearances:** The founder brings out key executives, major partners, or early customers to give testimonials and build unquestionable authority.
        
    - **The "Retail Push":** Because the legal limits are much higher, the founder is pitching to both everyday retail investors and institutional funds simultaneously. The stream is a highly orchestrated event designed to push the momentum over the finish line.
        
- **Post-Stream:** Ongoing SEC reporting. The company operates much like a public company, and the investors expect regular, highly professional updates.
