### The United States: SEC Regulation Crowdfunding (Reg CF)

In the US, the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) divides the crowd into two categories: **Accredited Investors** (wealthy individuals or institutions) and **Non-Accredited Investors** (the general public).

**1. Accredited Investors**

- **The Law:** If an individual is an accredited investor (typically defined as having a net worth over $1 million excluding their primary residence, or an individual annual income over $200,000), **there are no SEC investment limits**. They can invest as much as they want.
    

**2. Non-Accredited Investors (The General Public)** For everyone else, the SEC enforces a sliding scale based on a benchmark of **$124,000** to protect everyday people from losing their life savings on high-risk startups. The calculation applies to all Reg CF investments made across _any_ platform within a rolling 12-month period:

- **Scenario A: Lower Income or Net Worth**
    
    - _Condition:_ If **either** the investor's annual income **or** their net worth is _less_ than $124,000.
        
    - _The Limit:_ They can invest whichever amount is greater: **$2,500** OR **5%** of the greater of their annual income or net worth.
        
- **Scenario B: Higher Income and Net Worth**
    
    - _Condition:_ If **both** their annual income **and** their net worth are _equal to or more_ than $124,000.
        
    - _The Limit:_ They can invest up to **10%** of the greater of their annual income or net worth. However, the absolute maximum they can legally invest across all Reg CF platforms in a 12-month period is capped at **$124,000**
