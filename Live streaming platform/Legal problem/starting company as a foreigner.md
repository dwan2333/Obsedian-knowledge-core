- **Cost:** ~$50–$125 per year.
    
- **Action:** Sign up for the service first; you need their name and address for your filing.

2, **Draft your "Certificate of Incorporation":** Download the standard "Stock Corporation" template from the [Delaware Division of Corporations](https://corp.delaware.gov/corpformscorp09/).

- **Crucial Customization:** To have your Class A (1 vote) and Class B (10 votes) structure, you **cannot** use the simple 1-page form. You must draft an attachment (Article IV) that defines the two classes, their par value (use **$0.00001**), and their specific voting rights.
- Only issue **8 million** shares total to the founders. This leaves **2 million** (20%) unissued in the "bucket" to use for future investors and employee stock options without needing new paperwork.
-  Have a low "Par Value" (like **$0.00001**) and your company doesn't have millions in assets yet, your tax bill will drop to the minimum of **$450/year**.

**3, Example of Certificate of incorporation** 
**ARTICLE I: NAME** The name of the corporation is **[Your Company Name], Inc.** (the “Corporation”).

**ARTICLE II: REGISTERED OFFICE AND AGENT** The address of the Corporation’s registered office in the State of Delaware is **[Address of the Agent you selected, e.g., Northwest Registered Agent]**, and the name of its registered agent at such address is **[Agent Name]**.

**ARTICLE III: PURPOSE** The purpose of the Corporation is to engage in any lawful act or activity for which corporations may be organized under the General Corporation Law of Delaware (the “DGCL”).

**ARTICLE IV: CAPITAL STOCK** The total number of shares of all classes of stock that the Corporation has authority to issue is **10,000,000** shares, consisting of:

1. **8,000,000** shares of **Class A Common Stock**, $0.00001 par value per share.
    
2. **2,000,000** shares of **Class B Common Stock**, $0.00001 par value per share.
    

**A. Voting Rights:**

- **Class A:** Each holder of Class A Common Stock is entitled to **one (1) vote** for each share held.
    
- **Class B:** Each holder of Class B Common Stock is entitled to **ten (10) votes** for each share held.
    

**B. Voluntary Conversion:** Each share of Class B Common Stock is convertible into one (1) share of Class A Common Stock at the option of the holder at any time.

**C. Mandatory Conversion:** If any share of Class B Common Stock is sold or transferred to a person who is not a "Permitted Transferee" (as defined in the Bylaws), such share shall automatically convert into one (1) share of Class A Common Stock.

**ARTICLE V: BOARD OF DIRECTORS** The business and affairs of the Corporation shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board of Directors. The number of directors shall be determined as set forth in the Bylaws.

### **THE "LEGAL ARMOR" SECTIONS**

**ARTICLE VI: EXCULPATION (The "Mistake Shield")** To the fullest extent permitted by the DGCL (including Section 102(b)(7)), no **director or officer** of the Corporation shall be personally liable to the Corporation or its stockholders for monetary damages for breach of fiduciary duty as a director or officer.

- _Note: This protects you personally from being sued for your own money if you make a good-faith business mistake._
    

**ARTICLE VII: INDEMNIFICATION (The "Bill Payer")** The Corporation shall indemnify, to the fullest extent permitted by law, any person who was or is a party to any proceeding because they are or were a **director or officer** of the Corporation. This includes the advancement of legal expenses.

**ARTICLE VIII: FORUM SELECTION (The "Home Court")** Unless the Corporation consents in writing to an alternative forum, the **Court of Chancery of the State of Delaware** shall be the sole and exclusive forum for any internal corporate claims or any action asserting a breach of fiduciary duty.

**4, The Federal Level (The IRS)**

Delaware knows you exist, but the U.S. federal government does not. You cannot open a bank account or process secondary market trades without a tax ID.

- **Apply for the EIN (Employer Identification Number):** * As a non-U.S. resident, the online IRS portal will block you. You must download **IRS Form SS-4**.
    
    - Leave the SSN field blank and write **"FOREIGN"**.
        
    - Fax the completed form to the IRS international unit at **+1-304-707-9471**.
        
    - It typically takes 4 to 8 weeks to receive your official confirmation letter (the CP-575), which you will need for the bank.

**5,  Internal Governance (The Paper Trail)**

The state of Delaware does not keep a public record of your CEO, your internal rules, or your board of directors. You must generate and sign these documents internally and store them in a digital "Corporate Binder."

- **Action of the Sole Incorporator:** The person who filed the paperwork signs a document officially appointing the initial Board of Directors (which will be you).
    
- **Adopt the Bylaws:** This is the 20-to-30-page internal rulebook detailing how shareholder meetings are run, how board votes work, and how officers are appointed.
    
- **Initial Board Resolutions:** As the Director, you sign a document that:
    
    1. Appoints the corporate officers (CEO, President, Secretary, Treasurer).
        
    2. Authorizes the company to open a specific bank account.
        
    3. Officially authorizes the issuance of the company's shares.

**6,   Equity & IP Execution**

This is where you legally distribute the ownership and ensure the company actually owns the platform you are building.

- **Sign Restricted Stock Purchase Agreements (RSPA):** To execute the  founder split, each of the four founders must sign an RSPA. This contract issues the specific number of shares (Class B for you, Class A for the others) and outlines the 4-year vesting schedule with a 1-year cliff.
    
- **File the 83(b) Elections:** **This is a strict legal deadline.** Every founder must print, sign, and physically mail an 83(b) election form to the IRS within **30 days** of signing their RSPA. When mailing this internationally, always use registered mail and keep the tracking receipt forever.
    
- **Sign IP Assignment Agreements:** Every founder must sign a Confidential Information and Invention Assignment Agreement (CIIAA). This legally transfers all the code, algorithms, and business plans for the streaming and crowdfunding infrastructure from the founders as individuals to the Delaware C-Corp. Without this, investors will not touch the company.

**7,  The Financial Layer**

Once the IRS issues the EIN, you can activate the financial side of the business.

- **Open the Corporate Bank Account:** Use a tech-focused platform like Mercury or Brex, which allows for remote, international onboarding without needing a physical U.S. branch visit.
    
- **Fund the Par Value:** The founders cannot receive shares for free. If the par value is $0.00001, each founder must wire their specific buy-in amount (e.g., $28.00 for a 28% stake of 10 million authorized shares) from their personal bank account into the new corporate bank account.
    
- **File the W-8BEN-E:** Submit this form to your U.S. bank to certify the foreign status of the beneficial owner, which protects the company under international tax treaties and prevents automatic 30% withholding on certain revenues.
