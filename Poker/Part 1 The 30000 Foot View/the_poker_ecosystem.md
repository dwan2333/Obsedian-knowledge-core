# The Poker Ecosystem

*Companion document to [Part 1 — The 30,000-Foot View](<Part 1 The 30000 Foot View (Main).md>)*

## Overview

Before learning strategy, Miller maps the terrain. No-limit hold 'em is not a single game — it exists in three distinct formats, each with a different competitive environment, player pool, and earning potential. Choosing the right format to focus on is itself a strategic decision.

---

## Live Cash Games

The bread-and-butter format for most developing players. Games are stratified by stake level, and each tier has a distinct character:

| Stake | Level | Player Profile |
|---|---|---|
| **1-2 / 1-3** | Entry | Recreational players; loose, passive tendencies |
| **2-5 / 5-10** | Semi-Pro/Pro | More aggressive; tighter ranges; higher baseline skill |

- **1-2 / 1-3 games** are the most common entry point. They are filled with recreational players who play too many hands and rarely fold to pressure — this is your primary hunting ground as a developing player.
- The goal at small stakes is not to outplay opponents with elaborate bluffs; it is to **exploit weak ranges** by value-betting relentlessly.
- Live games run slowly (~25-30 hands/hour), which means individual decisions matter more but volume takes longer to build.

---

## Online Cash Games

- **Hyper-competitive** at all stake levels compared to live games — players have access to solvers, databases, and coaching material.
- Games run at **high volume and speed**, rewarding players who can make sound decisions quickly across multiple tables.
- **Unlimited earning potential** — volume is unconstrained compared to live play.
- Not recommended as a starting point if you are building fundamentals; live 1-2 is more forgiving and more profitable per hand for an exploitative player.

---

## Tournaments

| Format             | Character                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| Live Tournaments   | Social; high variance; luck plays a larger short-term role |
| Online Tournaments | More forgiving variance over volume; faster feedback loops |

- Tournaments require a distinct skill set — **ICM (Independent Chip Model)**, stack-size adjustments, push/fold theory — that is separate from cash game fundamentals.
- Part 1 (and the book as a whole) focuses on **cash game strategy**. Tournament play is a different course.

---

## Where to Focus

Miller's recommendation for a player starting out:

> **Live $1-2 cash games.** The player pool is weakest, the buy-in is manageable, and the exploitative strategy taught in this book is most effective here.
