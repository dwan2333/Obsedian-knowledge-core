## What This Part Covers

Part 2 is where the book stops philosophizing and starts teaching specific skills. Miller argues that beating **$1-2 live no-limit** requires exactly three post-flop disciplines layered on top of solid pre-flop play — nothing more, nothing less. Each is framed as a standalone "skill" that an improving player can drill in isolation:

1. **[Skill #1 — Play a Simple and Effective Pre-Flop Strategy](skill1_pre_flop_strategy.md)** — pick hands with equity-when-called, never limp, respect position.
2. **[Skill #2 — Don't Pay People Off](skill2_dont_pay_people_off.md)** — fold medium-strength hands when opponents commit their stack on turn or river.
3. **[Skill #3 — Assess Your Hand Value](skill3_assess_hand_value.md)** — extract the maximum from worse hands by counting "streets of value."

Part 2 closes with **[12 hand quizzes](hand_quizzes_1_2.md)** that drill all three skills in combination. You should be able to beat a typical 1-2 game with these three skills alone — Parts 3 and 4 only add layers once this foundation is automatic.

## The Three-Skill Interlock

The skills aren't independent — they lock together mathematically:

| Skill           | Role       | What it gets you                                            |
| --------------- | ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| **#1 Pre-Flop** | Structural | Enter pots with a **stronger range** than your opponents'   |
| **#3 Value**    | Offensive  | **Extract maximum value** when that range hits              |
| **#2 Folding**  | Defensive  | **Don't pay off** when opponents' narrower range hits *you* |

> Stronger hand more often + max value when ahead + no payoff when beat = a structural edge that's hard for a typical 1-2 field to overcome.

The quizzes are designed around this interlock. Quizzes 6 & 7 in particular are the centerpiece: **same hand, same board, opposite action** — a check from villain signals weakness (bet), a shove signals strength (fold). Miller calls this "the core logic of superior no-limit play."

## Key Takeaways

- **No limping, ever.** If you play a hand, you raise it. This one rule separates winners from losers at 1-2.
- **Suitedness is the gatekeeper.** Most non-pair offsuit hands are unplayable; suited connectors are among the best bluffing hands in the deck.
- **Tight is right.** ~14% of hands from early position, ~22% from CO, ~33% from BTN. Most opponents play far more — that gap is your edge.
- **Big bets on the turn and river nearly always mean what they look like.** Fold.
- **Flop bets are not the same as turn/river bets.** Small and medium flop bets are posturing, not strength — don't treat them as Skill #2 fold spots.
- **Count streets of value before you bet.** A hand worth one street ≠ a hand worth three, and sizing must reflect the plan.
- **In loose games, bet top pair until someone raises.** The "scary turn card" fear loses more money than any hero-call.
